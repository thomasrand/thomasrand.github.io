How to Get Here


Flying In

The main airport to fly into is JFK International, but if you can fly Southwest or U.S. Airways, MacArthur Airport located in Islip, NY is a closer option to Greenport.


For those flying from the west coast, another option is to fly to Green Airport located in Providence, RI. From here you would need to drive to the Cross Island Ferry in New London, CT. See driving directions from Boston.




Coming from New York (West)

From the tri-state area, take the Long Island Expressway (I-495) to Riverhead (Exit 73) and then Sound Ave / Middle Rd (Rt. 48) the rest of the way out. Both hotels are located on this main road. Keep an eye out for traffic on the LIE, as you can use the Grand Central Parkway as an alternative route.


Alternatievly, you can take the Long Island Railroad, which will bring you out to Greenport via the Ronkokoma branch.




Coming from Boston (North)

The best way to get to Long Island is via the Cross Sound Ferry located in New London, CT. The ferry can be accessed by either car or Amtrak. If you are driving, and the traffic is heavy, take the I-90 route instead of I-93.


If you are taking your car on the ferry be sure to book reservations a week or so early. If you are walking on the ferry be sure to arrange someone to pick you up in Orient as there isn't much public transportation besides a bus that runs to Greenport.

Attractions & Events

If you have free time there is plenty to do in the area for all ages. Here are a few things we recommend:

Vineyards

Take your pick. The north fork of Long Island is regionally well known for its vineyards. Really fun for an afternoon drink. Our favorites are:

  • Kontokosta - Great wine. Best view. Such great bluffs.
  • Croteaux - All rosès. Quaint, french feel.
  • McCall - Located on a farm. Great reds. Food truck visits on Fridays.
  • Sparkling Pointe - All sparkling wine. Fancy location.

Breweries

Relatively new, Greenport Brewery has two locations; a small (original) brewery in Greenport and a larger facility in Peconic. Great beer.


Greenport

Historic and quaint. Greenport is an old fishing village turned hotspot, attracting visitors with seafood, harbor views, and maritime-themed shopping.

Golf

Visit Island's End Golf & Country Club to check out the local links. Its a forgiving course until the 16th hole that runs along the sound. Don't slice.


The Hamptions & South Fork

Go here for some of the best beaches in the world and shopping. Go to Montauk for the lighthouse and beach. Go to East Hampton for shopping.
